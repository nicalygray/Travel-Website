discover : restaurants

Everything from light blondes to dark smokey french roasts. Seattle is known for it's coffee. Everything from the Starbucks to the small local bakery sidewalk cafe.

People in Seattle consume more coffee than in any other American city; one study stated that there are 35 coffeeshops per 100,000 residents and that Seattle people spend an average of $36 a month on coffee. It is nearly impossible to walk past a single block in a commercial area in Seattle without walking past at least one coffee shop. Coffee drinkers can get coffee at a local sidewalk stand, parking lot, tiny coffee houses, big coffee houses, drive-through, and even delivery.

2014 TOP 5 SEAFOOD RESTAURANTS

1. AQUA BY EL GAUCHO - 2801 Alaskan Way, Pier 70

AQUA by El Gaucho offers striking views of Elliott Bay and the Olympic Mountains. From the bar, you can even see the Space Needle. The menu of quality, and pricey, seafood includes appetizers ranging from pancetta-wrapped prawns and spicy crab bisque sparked with a splash of Johnnie Walker Red to a selection of local oysters. It's perfectly acceptable to order steak --- the kitchen knows its beef as well as its fish. analogcoffee.com

2. BLUEACRE SEAFOOD - 1700 7th Ave

Chef Kevin Davis and his wife Terresa are gracious hosts, serving up seafood sourced from both coasts, as well as the Gulf, in a way that's accessible to everyone. The chef’s Louisiana roots are also proudly displayed in one of the best po’ boys this side of New Orleans, not to mention the smoky braised collard greens and golden cakes made with lump crab. Don't miss the annual Maine lobster feast each summer, complete with traditional bibs. broadcastcoffee.com

3. THE BROOKLYN - 1212 Second Ave.

The Brooklyn is often bustling with guests so it can take some time to be seated without a reservation. Consider sidling up to the bar or counter where you’ll have a view of oysters being shucked (reportedly 400 dozen each week) and drinks being poured. Upholstered booths and dark wood furnishings give this spot a classy men’s club vibe. Located in a nineteenth-century building, The Brooklyn also has a loyal clientele of well-heeled concert-goers who attend performances at nearby Benaroya Hall. caffevita.com

4. CHANDLER'S CRABHOUSE - 901 Fairview Ave N.

It's hard to beat the combination of sparkling Lake Union vistas with choice seafood. The upscale ambience and nautical touches make this fish house popular for business meetings as well as for special occasions. It's also famous for its year-round crab fest, which features (in season) giant Tasmanian crabs, Florida stone crabs, Chesapeake Bay blues, and from the Pacific Northwest, Dungeness and Alaskan king crabs. espressovivace.com

5. ETTA'S SEAFOOD - 2020 Western Ave

Etta's Seafood has the same signature quality as Tom Douglas' other dining destinations (Dahlia Lounge, Lola, Palace Kitchen and Serious Pie), but here, the focus is on seafood. Brightly hued tiled mirrors line the walls, iron sculptures shaped like fish bones serve as coat hooks, and whimsical light fixtures evoking octopus tentacles illuminate the dining room. Douglas’ eclectic culinary vision borrows from points East and West to create a menu heavily accented with comfort food favorites. herkimercoffee.com
